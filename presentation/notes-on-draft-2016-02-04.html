Error rates

The manuscript provides no error rates for the classification of genes as mono or biallelically expressed.

  1. frequentist approach
    • \(p\)-values based on the null distribution of \(S_g^{(i)}\)
    • FDR control based on estimate of fraction of monoallelically expressed genes
  2. Bayesian approach
    • probabilities of mono/biallelic expression: \(\pi(m) + \pi(b) = 1\)
    • prior prob. based on
      1. estimate of fraction of monoallelically expressed genes
      2. distance from known imprinted genes \(\pi(m | \mathrm{dist})\) (further extension: HMM)
    • posterior \(\pi(m | x, y)\) given
      1. expression data \(x\) (RNA-seq)
      2. genotype data \(y\) (SNP-array)
      3. likelihood \(f(x,y|m)\) for \(m\) based on \(x,y\)
      4. prior \(\pi(m)\)

Notes

In the frequentist approach we only need the likelihood function \(f(x,y|b)\), whereas in the Bayesian one we also need \(f(x,y|m)\) (and the prior \(\pi\), of course).
